About Fluffdawg

Fluffdawg is a competitive Valorant player and 2023 NECC Collegiate Valorant Champion who has reached Radiant (the highest rank in the game), on multiple occasions.

With a 1.67 K/D ratio, Fluffdawg sits well above the average player, a testament to the consistency and discipline behind his training regimen. His aim routine has been refined through roughly 2,500 hours of deliberate practice and competitive play at the highest level.

This website breaks down the exact warm-up and aim training routine Fluffdawg uses to keep his mechanics sharp — from strafing drills to deathmatch exercises — so you can add the same structure to your own grind.

The Aim Routine
Step 01
Strafing
Begin by choosing a robot, and practice strafing left and right while keeping your crosshair at head level. This builds the muscle memory for counter-strafing and maintaining accurate crosshair placement while moving. Start slow, then gradually increase your movement speed while keeping your aim steady.
~1 min
Step 02
Strafing pt. 2
Now start to shoot 2-3 bullet bursts while strafing. This adds the complexity of recoil control and timing your shots, which is crucial for landing headshots in real matches. Aim for the closest bot, but try to kill the one you're strafing around.
~2 min
Step 03
Hard difficulty bots
Go onto the hardest difficulty and practice tracking the bots as they move unpredictably. This simulates real opponents who won't just stand still, and forces you to maintain smooth crosshair movement and adjust to erratic patterns. Focus on keeping your crosshair on the head as much as possible, even if you miss a few shots. The goal is to build the habit of tracking heads, not just spraying bullets. Aim for 20+ kills on hard bots before moving on.
~5 min
Step 04
Team Deathmatch
Go ahead and play 2-3 team deathmatches against real opponents. Don't worry about your K/D or trying to get kills — just focus on maintaining good crosshair placement, practicing your movement and strafing, and winning duels. This is the most important part of the routine, as it bridges the gap between controlled aim drills and the chaos of real matches.
~25 min
Step 05
Ranked
Now you're warmed up and ready to queue ranked. The goal here isn't to have a perfect K/D or carry the game — it's to stay mentally fresh, review your crosshair placement, and identify any bad habits in your aim or movement. Focus on playing your best, but don't get discouraged if you lose duels or have an off game. The real win is showing up consistently and putting in the work to improve over time. Play a max of 3 ranked games after your warm-up, then take a break and review your gameplay.
~1-2 hrs
Step 06
Review
After your session, take 10-15 minutes to review clips of your deaths and missed shots. This is where the real improvement happens, as you can identify patterns in your mistakes — whether it's poor crosshair placement, bad movement, or missed opportunities. Focus on learning from each death rather than getting frustrated. Over time, this will help you develop better habits and a deeper understanding of your own gameplay.
15 mins

Pro Tips From Fluffdawg
01
Consistency beats intensity A 45-minute focused routine every day will outperform a 3-hour session once a week. Build the habit before you build the volume.
02
Crosshair placement is everything Aim training only pays off if your default crosshair position is at head level. Do NOT sweep the floor with your crosshair.
03
Don't skip the 3 team deathmatches Aim trainers are awful, real opponents move unpredictably. These team deathmatches bridge the gap between controlled drills and live game scenarios.
04
Review your deaths After every session, watch back clips where you lost duels. 9 times out of 10 it's a crosshair placement or movement issue — not a reaction time issue.
